Host Safety & Security Guide

Comprehensive safety practices for parking and storage space hosts

Introduction

Welcome to ParknStore's comprehensive Safety & Security Guide for hosts. Whether you're listing a parking space or storage area, your safety and peace of mind are our top priorities. This guide provides essential information to help you create a secure, positive experience for both you and your renters.

Security Measures for Parking Spaces

  • Use temporary access codes instead of physical keys when possible
  • For gated areas, set specific access hours that work for your schedule
  • Clearly disclose any security cameras monitoring the area
  • Require renters to provide vehicle information (make, model, license plate)
  • Regular visual inspections of the parking area

Security Measures for Storage Spaces

  • Use high-quality locks (consider smart locks for access tracking)
  • Maintain control of access - you should be able to enter if needed
  • Establish specific access hours that work with your schedule
  • Require renters to provide a basic inventory of stored items
  • Prohibit storage of hazardous, illegal, or perishable materials

Prohibited Items for Storage

Never allow renters to store these items:

  • Flammable or combustible materials (gasoline, propane, chemicals)
  • Firearms, weapons, or ammunition
  • Illegal substances or drug paraphernalia
  • Perishable food items
  • Living plants or animals
  • Hazardous waste or materials

Final Tips for Success

  • Start Small: Begin with short-term rentals to build confidence
  • Meet Renters: When possible, meet renters in person for the first exchange
  • Trust Your Instincts: If something feels wrong, don't proceed
  • Stay Informed: Keep up with ParknStore policy updates and best practices
  • Communicate Clearly: Misunderstandings are the root of most issues

This guide provides general advice only. Local laws and regulations may vary. ParknStore recommends consulting with insurance professionals and legal advisors to understand your specific obligations and protections as a host.
